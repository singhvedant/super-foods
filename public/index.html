What is SuperFood ?

The term “superfood” is a fairly new term referring to foods that offer maximum nutritional benefits for minimal calories. They are packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. No standard criteria or legal definitions classify any food as a superfood at this time and it is nothing more than marketing jargon. However, most superfoods are plant-based.

Examples of popular superfoods include salmon, kale, açaí berries, kefir and almonds, just to name a few. Since there is not a strict definition of superfoods, any food that is nutrient-dense is commonly put into this category.

“Superfoods” is a catchword that has found popularity among the most health-conscious consumers. However, the concept of these nutritionally dense and healthy foods only focuses on one aspect of the complex global food system. The growing consumption of “superfoods”, such as quinoa or avocado, is having a growing environmental and social impact worldwide that is not being taken into consideration.

Advantages

These have several health benefits

  1. Their high vitamin and mineral content can help your body ward off diseases and keep you healthier.
  2. When incorporated into a well-balanced diet, these foods can promote heart health, weight loss, improve energy levels and even reduce the effects of aging.
  3. Fiber, which is also found in many superfoods, can help prevent diabetes and digestive problems, while phytochemicals have numerous health benefits including reducing your risk of developing cardiovascular diseases.
Disadvantages

Consumerism and exploitation

  1. A downside of superfoods is that the title alone may cause people to focus on a few specific foods, blinding them to other equally nutritious options that aren’t as hyped. This can have negative effects on ecosystems.
  2. Taking avocado as an example, we can clearly see the imbalance in the supply chain. Michoacan in Mexico produces 5 out of 10 avocados produced globally. Intensive production has caused biodiversity loss, extreme weather conditions, extensive soil degradation and has even been associated with increased earthquakes in the area

Solution

While superfoods provide a useful shorthand for the most nutritious foods available, very often locally sourced food can be equally nutritional. Variety in our diet is important not only to gain the benefit of eating a wide array of essential vitamins and minerals, but also to prevent one from eating too much (or too little) of a particular nutrient. It also keeps our meals interesting and flavorful.

With experts increasingly raising the alarm that our food system is broken, we need a different narrative which captures not only the role of food in nourishing our bodies, but also the connections between agriculture, the environment, and farmers’ livelihoods Awareness of the environmental impact of what we consume is the first step to reducing the climate impact of our food.
